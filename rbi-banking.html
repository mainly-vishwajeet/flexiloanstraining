RBI & Banking Fundamentals

Understanding the foundation of Indian banking and financial system

RBI & Money Concept

Key Principle

All money belongs to RBI (Reserve Bank of India)

Money is NOT a product

Money cannot be sold as per RBI regulations

Money can only be transacted

All money movements must be through proper transactions

Money can be lent and repaid

Lending and repayment are allowed financial activities

Selling money is NOT ALLOWED

As per RBI rules, money cannot be treated as a commodity

BFSI Framework

Money transactions are allowed only in these areas under RBI:

1. Banking

All banks operating under RBI guidelines

  • Public Sector Banks
  • Private Sector Banks
  • Foreign Banks
  • Regional Rural Banks

2. Financial Services

NBFCs (Non-Banking Financial Companies)

  • Flexi Loans
  • Muthoot Finance

  • Other licensed NBFCs

3. Services Sector

Service-based businesses

  • Schools & Educational Institutions
  • Colleges & Universities

  • Restaurants & Food Services

4. Investments/Insurance

Investment and insurance services

  • Mutual Funds
  • Insurance Companies
  • Stock Brokers
  • Investment Advisors

Key Regulatory Bodies

IT (Income Tax)

IT department monitors and controls all financial activities

Key Responsibilities:

  • Monitor financial transactions
  • Tax compliance verification
  • Anti-money laundering checks
  • Financial activity auditing

NPCI (National Payments Corporation of India)

Manages banking transactions and digital payments

Services Managed:

  • UPI (Unified Payments Interface)
  • Net Banking transactions
  • IMPS, NEFT, RTGS
  • Other digital banking transactions

Payment Systems Evolution

ECS (Earlier System)

Legacy

Physical cheque-based system

NBFCs used physical cheques for loan distribution with manual processing

Cheque Contents:

EMI amount
EMI date
Transfer authorization
Customer signature
Critical Rule: If loan has 24 EMIs but customer has only 12 cheques ‚Üí NOT ELIGIBLE
VS

NACH (Current System)

Current

Modern e-mandate system

Digital system that replaced ECS with automated processing

How it works:

Works through E-mandate and E-sign
EMI amount automatically debited from bank account
No physical cheque required
Completely digital process

Advantages:

Faster
Secure
Paperless
Automated
